Silk Mona Lisa* - A Suzhou Embroidery Masterpiece

In 2006, Chinese embroidery artist and Suzhou Master Xue Jindi ingeniously created a stunning embroidered replica of Leonardo da Vinci's "Mona Lisa". Using ultra-fine silk thread and traditional "double-sided embroidery", she recreated the intricate details of the famous painting, making the stitches almost invisible. The work amazed art experts around the world and proved that Chinese embroidery can rival fine paintings in realism. The "Silk Mona Lisa" has been exhibited internationally, receiving acclaim for its extraordinary artistic value, detail, and craftsmanship.

Haute couture meets Chinese embroidery – Guo Pei's global fashion influence

Famous Chinese fashion designer "Guo Pei" brought Chinese embroidery to the global stage when singer Rihanna wore a magnificent yellow cape to the 2015 Met Gala. Embroidered with gold thread and intricate patterns, the gown took more than 50,000 hours to complete and showcased the exquisite craftsmanship of traditional Chinese embroidery. This event marked a worldwide fascination with Chinese embroidery, proving its importance in modern haute couture.

The Revival of the Dragon Robe

In 2018, the Palace Museum in Beijing launched a project to restore and reproduce the stunning robes of the Qing Dynasty using ancient embroidery techniques. Many of the original dragon robes worn by the emperors had faded over time. Experts and embroidery masters spent years studying and meticulously recreating these using gold thread and silk techniques. The revival of these lost techniques inspires the younger generation to appreciate and pass on this beautiful craft.
